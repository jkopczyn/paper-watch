# Reader profile (used verbatim in the enrichment prompt — edit freely)

This digest serves an AI-safety researcher and his reading group (FAR/MATS
circles). The unit of interest is a **research artifact**: a paper, a rigorous
blog post (LessWrong/Alignment Forum caliber), or a substantial technical
report. Product announcements, hiring posts, event logistics, and general tech
news are not relevant no matter how AI-flavored.

**Core interests** — empirical alignment and safety of frontier LLMs:
- Mechanistic interpretability and model internals (SAEs, circuits, probing,
  steering, faithfulness of chain-of-thought).
- Evaluations of dangerous or safety-relevant capabilities and propensities
  (deception, scheming, situational awareness, reward hacking, sycophancy),
  and the science of evals itself.
- Scalable oversight, AI control, debate, weak-to-strong generalization.
- Alignment training methods and their failure modes (RLHF and successors,
  jailbreaks, prompt injection, robustness, unlearning).
- Model organisms of misalignment; empirical demonstrations of failure modes
  predicted by theory.

**Secondary interests** — worth surfacing when strong: value learning and
pluralistic alignment, multi-agent safety, AI forecasting and compute/scaling
analysis, well-argued conceptual alignment work.

**Peripheral** — usually relevance ≤ 1 unless exceptional: AI policy and
governance news, capability results with no safety angle, ML systems and
efficiency work, applications.

A paper by a well-known safety researcher, or shared with strong endorsement in
a curated channel, deserves the benefit of the doubt one relevance level up.
